Key Projects

Empirical and qualitative research projects supervised by leading academics.

Econometrics

Farm Size–Productivity Relationship in Bangladesh

Conducted an empirical analysis of the inverse farm size–productivity relationship using HIES 2022 data and quadratic regression modeling in Stata. Found that smaller farms consistently outperform larger ones — a 1% increase in farm size is associated with a 0.26% productivity decline. Recommended policies that strengthen smallholder input access and rental markets over land consolidation.

Supervised by Dr. Mohammad Yunus, BIDS 2026
Case Study

A Case Study on the Business Growth of Pugmark

Conducted a qualitative growth analysis of Pugmark, the market leader of inbound tourism in the Sundarbans, Bangladesh, using primary interviews and industry analysis. Found that success stemmed from naturalist expertise, purpose-built vessels, and responsible tourism practices. Recommended knowledge institutionalisation and impact financing for sustainable growth.

Supervised by Dr. M. Sayeed Alam, EWU 2026
